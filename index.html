Climate Change

Climate change is a very controversial but important topic in the world today. Climate change is the sum of global warming and its effects, caused by the rising average temperature of the earth. Overwhelming scientific opinion argues that climate change is largely the result of human use of fossil fuels like oil, gas and coal.

Topics

Deforestation - Deforestation is when the forests and other woodland of the world are cut down to make the land available for other uses. Deforestation drives loss of habitats and extinction of species.

Air Pollution - Air Pollution occurs when harmful gases are released into the atmosphere. This pollution can cause disease, allergies and death to humans, and possibly to animals and other life forms as well.

Wildfires - Wildfires are naturall occurring events that are said to promote forest health. Increasing droughts have meant that wildfires have increased in number and spread more easily as a result.

Statistics

  • Around 31% of the world is covered by forests and this is under threat from deforestation.
  • In 2019 the Earth's core temperature was 0.95 degrees Celsius warmer than the 20th century average.
  • The sea level is rising on average by 3.3 millimetres per year.
  • The Arctic ice is decreasing by an average of 12.85% per decade.
